Pressing is an aggressive defensive tactic where a team attempts to win the ball back immediately after losing possession, or to force errors from the opposition in their own half. The intensity of a press is often measured using PPDA (Passes Allowed Per Defensive Action) — a lower PPDA indicates a more aggressive press. A PPDA below 7 is considered a very high press; above 12 indicates a passive or mid-block approach.

High pressing (or gegenpressing) involves the entire team hunting the ball collectively, often targeting the goalkeeper and centre-backs during their build-up phase. The goal is to compress space, force long balls, and win possession in dangerous areas. Successful pressing requires high fitness levels, coordinated triggers — specific cues that tell the team when to press, such as a back pass to the goalkeeper, a misplaced touch, or a ball played into a player facing their own goal — and disciplined off-ball positioning. A high pressure count in StatsBomb data combined with successful turnovers in the attacking third is a strong indicator that a pressing strategy was effective in a match.

There are distinct types of pressing by zone and intensity. The high press is applied in the opposition's defensive third, directly targeting the goalkeeper and centre-backs. This is the most ambitious and physically demanding form — it requires every outfield player to be in a coordinated shape. The mid press is applied between the halfway line and the opposition's defensive third — the team allows build-up to begin but closes down aggressively once the ball moves into the middle zones. The low press, or selective press, is applied only when the ball enters the attacking team's defensive zone — it is essentially a mid-block with moments of aggression rather than a sustained press.

Barcelona under Valverde in 2018/19 primarily used a mid-to-high press, particularly in the first half-hour of matches. The pressing triggers were usually a back pass to the goalkeeper or a ball played wide to a fullback with limited options. Messi, despite not being known for pressing volume, was tactically intelligent in cutting off passing lanes to force the ball into pressurised zones rather than directly winning it himself. Suárez was the more physically active presser in the front line.

In StatsBomb data, pressing effectiveness can be analysed by looking at the pressure count per zone — pressures in the attacking third are the most valuable and indicate a high line of engagement. Successful pressures (those that lead to a turnover within a few events) versus unsuccessful pressures reveal whether the press was coordinated and triggered well or whether it was disorganised and easy to play through. Teams that press frequently but unsuccessfully often leave spaces in behind that opponents can exploit with a single accurate pass through the line.

Counter-pressing (or gegenpressing) is the specific press that happens immediately after the team loses possession — typically within the first 5-6 seconds. The rationale is that in those seconds, the opponent is disorganised and the ball carrier often has limited options. A successful counter-press wins back possession in a dangerous area; even an unsuccessful one buys time for the team to reorganise into its defensive shape. High pressure counts in the 40-60 metre zone from the opponent's goal (roughly the middle third to the defensive third of the pressing team's perspective) in the seconds following possession loss are the strongest StatsBomb indicator of effective gegenpressing.

One key risk of aggressive pressing is the press trap or press escape. An opponent that plays a third-man combination — using a player to draw the press and a teammate positioned to receive the release ball in the vacated space — can play through the press and arrive in a great deal of space. Teams that press without a well-organised second line of defence are particularly vulnerable to these moments, and in StatsBomb data, a pressing team that concedes shots in transition after high-pressure sequences shows evidence of this tactical vulnerability.
